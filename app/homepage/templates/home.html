Welcome to Probability Calculator

Adding / Multiplying Probabilities

This calculator allows users to input probabilities and perform basic arithmetic operations like addition and multiplication on them. For instance, users might input probabilities of different events, and the calculator would compute the combined probability of certain combinations of these events.

Full probability

This calculator computes the probability of an event happening given all possible outcomes. In probability theory, the full probability typically refers to the sum of the probabilities of all possible outcomes of an experiment, which is usually 1.

Probability of at least one event

This calculator calculates the probability of at least one of several events occurring. It could be useful in scenarios where there are multiple possible outcomes, and the user wants to know the likelihood of at least one of these outcomes happening.

Bayes' formula

Bayes' formula, or Bayes' theorem, is a fundamental theorem in probability theory that describes how to update the probability of a hypothesis given evidence. This calculator involves users inputting prior probabilities and conditional probabilities to compute posterior probabilities based on new evidence.
